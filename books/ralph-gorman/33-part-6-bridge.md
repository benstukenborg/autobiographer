Jack Doobie was the closest thing Ralph ever had to a mentor — and it is notable that he did not find one until he was in his fifties. Ralph had no teachers who believed in him as a young man (the one who liked him still gave him a D). He had no professional network, no family tradition of learning. Everything he knew, he had taught himself from books. Jack offered something different: knowledge transmitted in person, in the field, by someone who took the time to point things out. When Ralph says Jack taught him how to look at the world outside a city, he is describing something more than mushroom identification. He is describing what it feels like to learn from someone who is generous with what they know.
