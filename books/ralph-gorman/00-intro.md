title: "Introduction"
---

Ralph Gorman dropped out of high school at fourteen. By his own account, he was a street kid from the Bronx — a gambler, a fighter, a boy nobody expected much from. He spent his twenties pushing carts in the garment center and driving a cab on the night shift. Nothing in his early life suggested what would come next.

At twenty-six, on a dare from a friend, he enrolled in night school. Within a decade, he had a degree in physics from NYU, a career in semiconductor research, and a life that bore no resemblance to the one he'd left behind. He went on to become one of the early experts in integrated circuit technology, consulted for companies across three continents, and eventually traded his lab coat for a pair of work boots — building a farm in Connecticut where he made wine, grew produce, and sold at farmer's markets into his eighties.

He married a woman he met on a beach in Far Rockaway. They raised two children in Manhattan. He buried her almost twenty years ago and kept going.

Ralph was ninety-two when these conversations were recorded. He is ninety-three now. What follows are his stories — told mostly in his own voice, with occasional editorial context to orient the reader. He is direct, funny, digressive, and unsentimental. He does not ask for sympathy and does not embellish. He tells you what happened.

His favorite phrase — one he returns to again and again — is this: "If it's something you wanna do and you have the wherewithal to do it, you can do it." This book is the proof.
